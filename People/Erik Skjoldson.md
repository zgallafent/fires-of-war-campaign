Erik Skjoldson was an academic and teacher in my home town. He was born in [[Skjoldhelm]] and moved to the [[Anachreon (nation)|Anachreon]] as part of a collaborative research program between the two nations. During my studies at the academy in [[Wicksbend]] I worked frequently with him; with both of us being immigrants he knew how to help me with through the prejudices in the academic system. He eventually moved to the [[Arcaneum]] proper for research. I have encountered him a few times since then as I moved between research sites. He has still always been friendly, but also very careful to never do anything that might indicate disloyalty to the Anachreon. When I brought him my experimental research proposing a connection between the arcane gates of [[Izure]] and the celestial realms worshipped by the [[Vidanya]], he told me to leave and never mention it again.

"Professor, do you have a minute?"
Professor Skjoldson turned back from the bookshelf to look out his office for, where I was knocking at the threshold. "Hazelmere! My good boy, of course! Come in, come in."
He placed a few books back on the shelf and sat behind his desk, shuffling vellum, papers, and folders aside to make space. I took the seat across from him.
"Please, forgive the mess. I wasn't expecting anyone today. I had just been looking through some of the older scraps from the old Wicksbend archive."
"Don't worry, Professor. I know this is probably a surprise, I was just putting the finishing touches on a piece I've been working and I wanted you to be the first to see it." I placed a freshly bound booklet in on the desk and pushed it towards him. 
"Of course! I'd love to see it." He took it and turned the cover around, then his smile dropped. "'The Heavens Gate and the Prime Deities: An Analysis of the Heavens Gate in Izuri Literature and as Related to the Prime Deities of the Vidanya'. What is this supposed to be, Hazelmere?"
"This is my research project I've been working on."
He sighed and set the book down. "You're always talking about this 'Heavens Gate'. We don't even know if it's real. I thought you said you were going to start focusing on something significant to the war."
"This is significant, Professor!" I felt my face flush with heat. "If I'm right, this changes everything about the war. Look, let me show you."
I flipped the book open to a page of translations, where I had spent days poring over scraps of text from various research sites. I've been cross-referencing all of these different references to the Heavens Gate and together they seem to indicate this went to an actual place; that it's called "Heavens" because you could actually reach the heavens through it."
"No, I've heard enough about this."
"Think about what this could mean though! If we could prove to the Vidanya that the arcane magics of the Izuri can actually send someone into the heavens, we could actually set foot there. It would completely change our relationship with them."
"Hazelmere..."
"If we can just find-"
"*Enough!*" Professor Skjoldson slammed his hand down on the book, breathing heavily. We watched each other for a few moments, neither sure of what to say. Then, he seemed to look at something beyond me, before continuing. 
"I don't want to hear any more about this Heavens Gate. The Anachreon does not deal with the divine, you know that."
"But-"
"No, that's the end of it. If you've ever taken my advice seriously, listen to me know. Destroy this, don't let anyone else see it. Nothing good will come of it."
I opened my mouth, ready to argue, but his eyes told me that it was a lost cause. It wasn't that he didn't believe me, or that he was angry. He looked like he was scared.
I pulled my research back and snapped it shut as I stood up. "I understand. I won't bother you anymore."
"Hazelmere, I'm sorry, I just-"
I didn't hear whatever he said after that, as I shut the office door on my way out.
