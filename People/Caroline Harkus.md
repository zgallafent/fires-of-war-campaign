Caroline Harkus is a [[Kithenir|Kithenir]] in [[Mystara]]. She is one of the leading members of the kithenir and involved with the efforts to rediscover the [[Izuri Gate System]].

As part of the Kithenir's ongoing quest to rediscover the history of the [[Izure]], Harkus spent many years in the [[Arcaneum]]. She worked as a professor teaching about artifice and technomancy, which she used as a cover while digging for information. Her most notable accomplishment was discovering how messages could be encoded and decoded based on the resonant frequency of magical objects, a technique now used for all secret communications in the [[Anachreon (nation)|Anachreon]] army.

Harkus became well acquainted with [[Hazelmere]] while working at the Arcaneum. They first met when Harkus intervened in an argument between Hazelmere and another student.

## Player Relationship
### Affection
**4/6**
While she was never officially an advisor to Hazelmere, Professor Harkus was one of the most supportive faculty through his time at the Arcaneum. She was one of the only people that always judged his work on its merit, not on his race. However, the level of decorum between students and professors that had to be respected, even when when Hazelmere was no longer truly a student in the Arcaneum, prevented him from calling ever really calling her a friend.
### Respect
**6/6**
Hazelmere never had many opportunities to see Harkus actually work magic. However, in the rare glimpses he did see, she always had an aura of confidence that only ever came with complete mastery. Her theoretical knowledge was unparalleled, though she always kept what she knew close to her chest.
### Trust
**6/6**
The Arcaneum is incredibly strict with state secrets. Rumors among the students always claimed that the Anachreon had ways of making sure you couldn't even remember their secrets if they thought you were overly permissive. When Hazelmere first joined the research team for the Izuri gates, he was told that sharing any of their research would be considered treason.

Hazelmere never intentionally shared anything about the gates with Harkus. However, he once accidentally dropped a page of his notes discussing them in her office and left it there. The next time he came by, Harkus slipped it back to him without a word. University rules required her to report what happened, but she never said a word.

Hazelmere wouldn't have survived so many years in the cutthroat competition of the Arcaneum, especially as an immigrant to the Anachreon, without Professor Harkus's constant guidance. Of all the things she did for him though, that one favor saved Hazelmere from the greatest mistake he could have made in his all of his time there.
## Stories
### The Arcaneum
I first met Professor Harkus in my entry level lessons when I arrived at the Arcaneum. She was on the lecturer circuit teaching about the basics of magical resonance from enchantments. I had just recently arrived at the Arcaneum and was still adjusting to becoming a refugee. I got into a heated debate between lectures with someone from the [[League of Salt and Silver]] over how much welfare the Anachreon should be providing to the people near our border with the [[Vidanya]]. They were arguing we went too much aid and I made some cruel personal attacks against them. Caroline happened to be nearby and intervened to break it up, taking me into her office to scold me.

After explaining to me in excruciating detail what was wrong with what I said, she gave me some advice that I'll never forget. "You're passionate about what you believe because you want to do good and kind things. However, you've also conjured up an idea that anyone disagreeing with you is against that goodness and kindness, which makes them the enemy. That attitude only ensures that they be your enemy."

Every few weeks after that, Professor Harkus pulled me into her office to discuss my studies. She had talked to [[Erik Skjoldson|Professor Skjoldson]] about me and was particularly interested in hearing about the [[Izure|Izuri]] artifacts I had been researching. She'd in exchange help me with with understanding more of the technical details of artifacts from my research that were beyond my education. 

I remember one meeting especially, where we were discussing the research expedition in the Vichon Order. When I explained how the inscription there described a [[Izuri Gate System|key]] that would unlock the secrets of how the Izuri traveled the land so quickly, she seemed very surprised, and began furiously scribbling notes. After I left, I stopped to talk to a classmate in the hall. A few minutes later, she rushed out of her office, writing a name on a letter as she walked away. 

I continued meeting with Professor Harkus for several years, before it came to an abrupt end. I knocked on her office door and it swung open to a dark room. Everything was still there, but the mage lights had gone out and the professor wasn't there. She'd never been gone when I came for our regular meetings. I stepped into the room and raised a light. Nothing in the room looked out of place except a single letter with my name on it, lying on the desk. The letter have a magical pulse as I touched it and the seal broke open. When I opened the letter, I found a small brass key, a silver crownmark, and a letter. 

> Hazelmere,
> I apologize for departing and leaving you with naught but a letter to explain. If I had been less discreet, the Arcaneum may have obstructed my departure. 
> When I was brought to taken out of my hometown, I was told this education was an honor to my family. I was promised I would be serving my country and doing good. As the years have passed, I've found that to be, not a lie, but a misrepresentation. Our research is used for the betterment of the Anachreon. However, not for the benefit of the people the Anachreon. You haven't seen this, the politics of the upper Arcaneum aren't shown to the lower stratum, but the whole of the Anachreon has been turned purely to the benefit of Crown and Concord. I cannot be a part of it any longer. 
> You have been a favored student of mine for so many years. I believe that you will discover many incredible things in your lifetime. Don't let it all be spent in service of a nation that does not return your dedication. The key I have left you will provide you with what you need when that moment comes. You will know how to use it. The crownmark is enchanted to let those who would be your friends recognize. Do not spend it frivolously. 
> I would tell you where I am going, but the risk is to great if someone else manages to break the enchantments protecting this letter. I do hope to see you again. 
> Caroline Harkus
