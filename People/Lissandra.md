Lissandra is an Elven woman that I met through the [[Anachreon (nation)|Anachreonan]]-[[Vidanya|Vidanyan]] trade caravans that passed through my home. We became friends, though our contact was very intermittent. I would tell her about the things I was learning in academy when she visited and she would take me out to explore the seedier parts of the city I never visited. We never discussed her religion, but I always had a sense that she didn't approve of some of the research I did into the arcane. The first time we fought was over the environmental effects of some of the [[Anachreon (nation)|Anachreon's]] arcane inventions. The last time I saw her, shortly before the war broke, she told me she hoped we'd stay friends regardless of our nationalities.

## Stories
### Argument About Magic
The first argument I ever had that involved religion nearly ended my friendship with Lissandra.

It wasn't the first discussion I ever had about the gods. My parents both followed the gnomish tradition of worshipping the Lovers through the Sehamite Rites and practiced them with perfect consistency up to my father's death. I learned the core myths of the Stormbringer and the Moonweaver from them as a child. However, the allure of the arcane pulled me away from their beliefs, and over time I slowly adopted the irreligion of the Anachreon.

Gods were rarely discussed in Wicksbend and I don't believe there was a single public temple in the city, with it being an academy town. There were only two days each year where it broke into the public consciousness: the Caravan Festivals. The elves passing through the city always brought a fervor of discussion about their culture, their lands, and especially their faith. Strange and oblique to the Anachreonans, the city was always mystified by the rituals they would hold in the forests beyond the city.

When I met Lissandra, we were both far too young to really pay attention to any of those things. However, as we grew older it became a subtle thread of tension in our friendship, something we never discussed but knew that we fundamentally disagreed on. We managed to preserve this uneasy truce up until two years before the Sacking of Wicksbend.

In the early summer, the first Caravan Festival of that year, we had been wandering aimlessly through the trails just beyond the city walls, catching up on a year's worth of news and changes. Lissandra had commented on now busy the city was, I attributed to the Tribunal shipping their new TAV fertilizer along the Odera. This led to an explanation of how the fertilizer is made, which is where the argument started.

TAV, or transmutation via non-acidic vitriols, was a new arcane process to create materials for fertilizer from apatite without using oil of vitriol. A cheaper, safer process meant more demand, so the Syndicate had authorized expanded mining production in the mountains along the Vidanyan border.

When I told Lissandra about the apatite mines, she was furious. She said that the mines had destroyed one of the sacred groves that get caravan always stops at to worship Melora. I became defensive, arguing that our need to feed people was more important than a special forest for praying to a god that doesn't even listen. After that, she quickly left to go back to her people, and I didn't see it hear from her again until the second Caravan Festival the next year.

Two years after that argument, instead of a Vidanyan trade caravan arriving at Wicksbend, it was an army. They marched in the name of the Wildmother, in revenge for her groves that had been destroyed. When they left, there wasn't a single stone of the academy left standing. Even now, decades later, the city is an empty testament to their god's wrath.
