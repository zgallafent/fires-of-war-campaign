Marlamin was Dan's character. He was a halfling rogue and generally the diplomat for the party. He wasn't always quick to speak but very insightful when he did. When [[Collins de Jarrag Iolaire|Collins]] confronted me after the [[01 - Escape from Bridgewater|battle]] at [[Bridgewater]], Marlamin was the one that finally talked Collins down. A mimic killed him while we were [[02 - Death Beyond the Gate|exploring]] the [[Wycrof Gate|Wycrof gate]].
# Player Relationship
## Affection
**5/6**
Marlamin has never seemed to understand discretion, especially with sensitive topics such as the gates or his resurrection and visions. However, this also means it's very easy for Hazelmere to understand the what and why of Marlamin's emotions. Regardless of how much he can trust Marlamin, Hazelmere can't help but like him.
## Respect
**6/6**
When they first met, Hazelmere definitely considered Marlamin to be the weak link of the group as far as skill in combat went. However, ever since the resurrection he's been far less confident in gauging Marlamin's abilities. Regardless of his abilities though, Marlamin now had a god walking beside him. It might be good it bad, but it was definitely something to be wary of 
## Trust
**1/6**
When they first met, Marlamin's word he'd be safe in the group was the only thing he could trust from among them. However, the Marlamin that's come back from the grave is very clearly not the same one that went in, and now he's completely devoted to his god. If Collins can't even fully trust Marlamin, how could Hazelmere?
