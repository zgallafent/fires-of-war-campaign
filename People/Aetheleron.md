Aetheleron is an [[Anachreon (nation)|Anachreonan]] researcher and my current academic master. He is leading research efforts into the [[Izuri Gate System]]. He was the headed researcher at the archaeological digs in [[Fiedorek]], the [[Vichon Order]], and [[Bridgewater]].

As a member of the [[Prismatica]], Aetheleron began researching the gates in an effort to better understand [[Fiedorum|fiedorum]], since the gates are the only known objects made from it. The college hopes to studying it to find ways to improve the durability and inertness of alchemical glass.

## Player Relationship
### Affection
**3/6**
Hazelmere has known Aetheleron for over two decades, first as an academic advisor and later as his lead researcher. Their relationship has always been one of master and apprentice, which had discouraged any kind of personal connection between the two.
### Respect
**5/6**
Hazelmere has had plenty of opportunities to see how capable Aetheleron is first hand in the last 20 years. He can work magic that Hazelmere can barely comprehend and there are few mages in the entirety of the Arcaneum that can equal him in knowledge or skill. However, there is a small part of Hazelmere that wonders, for how willing Aetheleron is to pass off Hazelmere's research as his own, how much can Hazelmere trust that Aetheleron's accomplishments aren't "collaborations" as well.
### Trust
**2/6**
Hazelmere was completely naive to the realities of the Arcaneum when he arrived. As Hazelmere's advisor and head research, Aetheleron has been one of the main mechanisms by which Hazelmere has been exposed to the machinations of the colleges.

When remembering the battle at Bridgewater, Hazelmere can't help but compare Aetheleron and [[Gurlurek]]. He's not sure how much Aetheleron would be willing to sacrifice for whatever interests he serves, but it clearly includes the people that work for him. Hazelmere is willing to give him one last chance to explain himself out of respect.

### Before Bridgewater
Aetheleron, Alexander, Ronald, and I met up the day before we left for Bridgewater to make sure we had all of our supplies and research materials prepared. We had managed to get funding through the Prismatica through the trip, enough to cover everything and let us replace a few outdated instruments. The funding had been a boon, to be sure, but Aetheleron hadn't told us the cost until after we agreed.

"All of our research notes?! You should have told us that weeks ago!" I threw the contract back at Aetheleron, papers spilling out of the binder and flying across the ground.
"It's not that important, we're just making a copy for them."
"I don't care if it's copy! I wouldn't have agreed if you had been up front about the terms."
"You can't back out of this now, we've already spent the money."
"I know, by the Throne I know. I'm not going to be the one dragged up in front out the council for this. It's a dirty thing for you to do, Aetheleron."
Alexander put his hands up and stepped between us. " Look, it's done. Let's just get through this trip and worry about it when we get back."
Aetheleron waved a hand, gathering up all of the pages with a quick down spell before holding it out to me. I scribbled my name at the bottom, the last of our four signatures, before turning and walking away from them to one of our carts. There wasn't anything left to pack, but adjusting the ropes would say last mean I didn't have to look Aetheleron in the eye longer. 
Ronald followed me back to the cart and set a hand on my shoulder. "Hey, what's going on? You've never had so much of a problem with the Prismatica before."
I pulled hard on a rope, the cart creaking as it strained against the wood. After a moment, I shook my head and sighed. "There's not adding specific about them. It's just..."
I glanced back behind me, making sure Aetheleron and Alexander were still talking before I continued. "Before [[Caroline Harkus|Professor Harkus]] left, she told me there's more going on with the colleges than we know. I can't figure out what it is exactly, I just feel like we can't trust them. Not completely."
Ronald didn't respond, just standing next to me. In the silence, I caught a bit of what Alexander was saying to Aetheleron, "-the selection should be right after we get back. If they make you Magistrate-"
Alexander cut off there and I heard the two of them walk over towards us. Aetheleron was the first to speak. "Hazelmere, everything's ready."
"Yes."
"Alright, then we all meet back here tomorrow. Get some rest." I heard him begin walking away, then stop. "I know you're not thrilled about this, but I promise, it's for the best. For all of us. I'll explain more soon, just please be patient with me "
That was the last time we ever talked about it.
