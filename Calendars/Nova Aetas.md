The Nova Aetas, or the New Age, is the calendar system invented by the [[Anachreon (nation)|Anachreon]]. It went into effect in 134 NA. The calendar is designed to pull all of the year into order through even cycles of months, weeks, and days. The current year in the Nova Aetas system is 463 NA.

# Description
As with all systems, a year in the Nova Aetas is 400 days long. Each year is divided into four seasons, each of which have four months of 25 days. In addition to months, the calendar has a system of 5-day weeks, which makes each month exactly five weeks long.

Each season, month, and weekday has a theme. The months are named for their themes, which virtues of civilization and scholarship.

# Background
>_Stone builds a hall. Memory preserves a people. But writing conquers time._

The Nova Aetas calendar was invented in 134 NA. The [[Arcaneum]] chose the origin year for the system based on two major milestones that occurred in the same year. Firstly, paper production was redeveloped. The Anachreon believes that paper was common place in the old empire and that much of it's success came from literacy, record keeping, and communication. Before the redevelopment of paper, literacy was rare (often just in the clerical orders, nobility, and adjacent mecantiles). There was also the ascendance of a leader at the Arcaneum who broke out from simply being the leader of a school and academy and aggressively spread the influence and political impact of the Arcaneum throughout the [[Creon Coast]] and beyond. The name of this leader is [[Eadric Vearon van Merrowe]]. This is the point in history that can be pointed to as the beginning of the Anachreon Empire vs just the Anachreon Arcaneum and it's academy.

The length of the week in the Nova Aetas is an explicit rejection by the Arcaneum of older religious week structures in favor of a more practical system with no irregularities. Each day of the week is given a name based on the academic process, along with which the students of the Arcaneum memorize:
>Observe. Analyze. Apply. Record.
