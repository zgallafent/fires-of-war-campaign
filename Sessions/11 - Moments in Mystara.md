After [[10 - Cards on the Table|welcoming us]] to [[Mystara]], [[Caroline Harkus|Harkus]] offered us a place to stay in the [[Kithenir|kithenir]]. She and [[Hazelmere]] needed a few weeks to study the [[Gate Almanac|gate almanac]], so we would need to spend some time in Mystara waiting. She also offered to prepare some tokens for us to help with the local language, [[Thalassian Coast|Thalassian]], since maybe people here wouldn't speak common.

[[Collins de Jarrag Iolaire|Collins]] asked Harkus if there were any weaponsmiths in the city he could commission and [[Marlamin]] asked if there are any shrines to [[Tymoro]]. Harkus called for a young woman named [[Kirharra]] and asked her to be a guide for them in the city. She told Collins that she knew a blacksmith named [[Zaiha]] near the wharf that we could trust. She also told Marlamin that he could show anyone a symbol of Tymoro and they would direct him, then sent us out into the city. Hazelmere stayed behind to start working with Harkus.

As we headed into the city, Marlamin had a vision where he was in the body of a man from early Mystaran history, when the city was first being founded. Marlamin experienced building the local Tymoran temple through the man. When the vision ends, Marlamin felt a strong sense of deja vu that he's been to Mystara already.

Kirharra led us down into the city and along the wharf Itsahauste. It was full of a variety of food and trade stalls. Across the cove at the Zaiharri pier, we could see an orc ship docked as it rendered down a whale it had brought in. Through her halting common, Kirharra helped Collins and Marlamin buy some local food to try.

## Marlamin
Marlamin split off from our group to head up a hill to where he could see the temple to Tymoro. Inside, [[Lorea]], the elderly priestess of the temple, approached him. She welcomed him to the Wayfarer's Rest, then said that she could see the Changebringer in him. When she asked what he was searching for, Marlamin answered that he needed guidance and communion. Lorea showed Marlamin two barrels of oil attached to a harness and asked him to carry them up to the beacon at the top of the temple. She said he would find his guidance in doing the work. 

Marlamin spent the next four days hauling oil, mucking stalls, chopping firewood, and cleaning the temple. Through his time at the temple, he could feel his mind clearing and sorting into place. On the fourth day, as he climbed up to the beacon, Lorea was there waiting for him. She asked if he had found his path yet and he answered no, only that he knew where he was going. Lorea asked him to look out over the city with her.

When Marlamin looked, he had another vision of Mystaran history. It was a different man than the first vision, standing at the same beacon, clad in armor and looking out over the ships of warriors in the cove that he had arrived with. The city was more developed compared to his first vision. It seemed to be the end of a war campaign and the man was revelling in having freed the land so that it could continue on worshipping the Changebringer forever. When the vision ended, Marlamin saw the ship of orcs in the harbor and felt strongly that he needed to join them so he could travel abroad. Lorea said that she could tell Marlamin is a do-er and would find his path in the doing. Marlamin told her about how Tymoro [[07 - Victims, Visions, And Vitals|brought him back to life]] and she said that meant he would definitely receive guidance from her soon. She then told him that he didn't need to come back to the temple tomorrow.


## Collins
After Marlamin went off to the temple, we continued on to see Zaiha in her smithy. Kirharra got her attention and they exchanged a few words in Thalassian, most of which seemed to be Zaiha yelling at her. Kirharra said that Zaiha could make weapons for Collins and not to be cheap. She then left with [[Ghithgod]].

Collins pulled out his scythes to show Zaiha. She said they were no good because they're too short. Then she asked what other weapons he's familiar with and pointed him to a wall of partially-made weapon blanks. Collins said he knows how to use longswords and showed his to get. Zaiha replied that it was better, but still no good. She asked for Collins to prove he can pay and he showed her a bag of gold coins, at which point she agreed to work with him.

Zaiha asked Collins to give a small performance with his longsword so she could gauge how proficient he is. She then asked what Collins wants out of a sword. He answered that he prefers to use two swords and doesn't care about their appearance at all, just their quality. Zaiha said that they would need good steel and zinc to make high quality swords. Collins pulled out the bag of holding and began to pile the various weapons we had [[05 - Ethically Acquired Antiquities|collected]] on a table, asking if any were high enough quality steel. Zaiha was more interested in the bag than the weapons and asked how he got it and who made it. She said that he would need the help of a mage like the one that made the bag if he wanted to make a magic sword. She agreed to smith one for him in exchange for 100gp and trading her all of the weapons we had, as well as bringing her some zinc.

Collins went to the docks and asked the port master [[Cael]] if he could get any zinc, who said it would cost 24gp. Collins asked if he could work for it instead and Cael agreed to trade some zinc in exchange for five days' work. He assigned Collins to help with sorting and hauling lumber. After the first day, the manager moved him to just grading the wood because of how well Collins knew lumber, thanks to his family's business.

## Ghithgod
After leaving Collins, Ghithgod told Kirharra that he wanted to find work with the orcs. She took him to the docks and pointed him at the port master Cael. He asked Ghithgod if he was Anachreonan and Ghithgod said yes. Cael told Ghithgod he'd have to leave any weapons behind in the port office and then directed him towards an orc further along the docks.

When Ghithgod approached the orc, he sized him up and left. He then asked if Ghithgod wanted work. The orc introduced himself as [[N'duka]] and asked if Ghithgod was Anachreonan. Ghithgod again answered yes and Nduka said he knew it because the Mystarans would never work for him. Nduka brought Ghithgod onto the ship, where the crew was working to render down a whale into oil.

They gave Ghithgod an axe and directed him to start cutting up the whale carcass into chunks. The work completely exhausted him and by the end of the day he collapsed, but he managed to impress Nduka with his work. He took Ghithgod down to join the crew in the mess hall. Ghithgod spent the next week working during the day on the ship and carousing with the crew in the evening. At the end of the week, Nduka tells Ghithgod that the crew will be going out sealing soon and offered a place for Ghithgod to join them.

## Back Together
After a week, we all met back up at the docks to discuss our plans. Hazelmere and Harkus had only gotten through a quarter of the almanac, so we planned to spend more time in Mystara. Collins asked if Hazelmere or Harkus could help with enchanting the swords Zaiha was making and they both agreed. Marlamin asked Ghithgod if he could go along on the orcs' sealing expedition. Ghithgod took him to meet Nduka, who asked if he was a cleric. Marlamin said yes and Nduka said they could bring him if he helped to keep the crew healed during the trip.

After Ghithgod and Marlamin left, Collins went up to the Wayfarer's Rest because he wanted to understand more about Tymoro. He met Lorea and they spent a while talking about her teachings.
