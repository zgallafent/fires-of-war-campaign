After [[Ashara]] and the [[Kivipuro|Kivipuros]] [[08 - The Night of Nattevagten|left]], we left the ruined house to make camp. We found out from talking to [[Marlamin]] that he had lost nearly all of his memories, and some of what he still remembered was wrong. He turned in for bed and the rest of us stayed up to discuss what happened. We all agreed that we were worried about the changes in Marlamin and that we would watch him. As we discussed the [[07 - Victims, Visions, And Vitals|shared vision]] we had, [[Reb Tevye]] told us that the tower [[Hollowspire]] near [[Wycrof]] was built by the [[Izure|Ancients]]. It used to be where the city council met, until the [[Velmon Arguillan|magistrate]] took power. He also said that the citadel in Wycrof was built on the bones of the Old Ones, and that the [[Stone Choir]] might be that old as well. He also told us about some of the members of the city council. After this, Reb Tevye went to bed. We continued discussing what our plans going forward would be, but couldn't come to a decision. That night, [[Collins de Jarrag Iolaire|Collins]] and [[Ghithgod]] had nightmares about our shared vision. 

In the morning, Marlamin told us that being dead had been like a dream, where he saw his family and [[Tymoro]]. He then repeated what Tymoro told him about the gates and that they needed to be destroyed.

He returned to Wycrof and Reb Tevye led us back to the Stone Choir. [[Ghilain|Brother Ghilain]] was waiting for us there and said Ashara had sent word of what happened. He told us about a similar ceremony he had experienced when he was a young cleric and mentioned that normally visions during the ceremony were previous experiences of members of the ceremony. We shared the names of the targets from the Nattevagten's black book with him. He and [[Hazelmere]] apologized to each other over the fight they had last time.

We then went into town. The guards there weren't checking papers anymore, but there was an increased presence and the were on high alert. Many people were leaving town to head west. When we reached the center square, we found a packed crowd surrounding a high platform. The magistrate was standing on it next to [[Jorren]], who was in a cage and covered in oil. He have a speech about how requires needed to be punished, then snapped his fingers and Jorren burst into flames. Some of the crowd cheered, while others were disturbed. Reb Tevye cried out and moved to heal Jorren, but a man feigning drunkardness wandering through the crowd had surreptitiously moved next to our cart and stopped him.

Once the crowd cleared, we continued on to the [[Laurel Crown Inn]], which was mostly filled with soldiers. We talked to [[Agathe Brelin]] for a bit and we toasted to Jorren together. She told us she could get us two weeks of rations and horses in a day or so if we were planning to leave town. We settled into some rooms upstairs, where a barmaid came to tell us a young girl had come to the inn looking for us. Hazelmere] went down to the main room and met [[Igrid]], who told him that he should stop by [[Harkus Hunt and Haggle]] tomorrow. Meanwhile, Marlamin told Collins more about his vision and reiterated the need to destroy the gates, as well as expressed his distrust of Hazelmere. Reb Tevye went back to his home in the Cut and we turned in for the night.

In the morning, [[Hank]] told us he didn't want to get any more involved in these high-stakes battles over gates and national intrigue. He left to find a trading vessel in [[Basilique]] that could give him passage home. We then went to Harkus Hunt and Haggle. Igrid was at the counter when we arrived and led us into a back room. She left, and after a while [[Osbert Harkus|Osbert]] came into the room. He took us into a storage room and pulled a rug away to reveal a trapdoor. The room below had a teleportation circle, which uniquely did not require any kind of talisman to operate. Osbert told us that someone was waiting on the other side to meet us and stepped out. Hazelmere and Marlamin briefly debated whether to go through, and after Hazelmere convinced him we all stepped into the circle.
