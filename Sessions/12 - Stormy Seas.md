As we at met in the city after a [[11 - Moments in Mystara|week apart]], [[Ghithgod]] and [[Marlamin]] mentioned that [[N'duka]] had put out a call for more crew. [[Caroline Harkus]] had told [[Hazelmere]] that he would need to leave for a week or so while the [[Kithenir]] conducted some rituals, so he was inclined to come. As Marlamin was walking away, [[Collins de Jarrag Iolaire|Collins]] had a brief vision of a woman walking next to him. She was barefoot with a dress and long, flowing golden hair. Collins stopped Hazelmere from leaving and described what he saw. Hazelmere recognized it as the classical depiction of the goddess [[Tymoro]], who was often shown to have long golden hair to represent the many paths she walked. They both agreed they should go out on the sealing trip with Marlamin and Ghithgod to keep an eye on him. 

Collins and Hazelmere approached N'duka together about joining the crew. He recognized Collins from his work with [[Cael]] on the docks handling lumber shipments and Hazelmere explained he was a skilled navigator, so N'duka accepted both of them on the trip. The ship departed the next day, sailing west and then south along the [[Thalassian Coast]]. N'duka had a handmade map with the hseal hunting grounds marked out west of [[Straag Bohumin]], though his maps had few landmarks and fewer details. Hazelmere used the maps he had brought from the [[Arcaneum]] to help N'duka update his own maps, which cut over a day off of the travel time. 

As we were sailing, Collins pulled Marlamin aside privately to tell him about the woman he had seen in his vision. Marlamin asked what she looked like, then explained that when he had [[07 - Victims, Visions, And Vitals|seen]] Tymoro, she had dark skin and hair.

A short while after we arrived at the hunting grounds, we stopped a storm coming on across the horizon. We had some time before it hit to work on preparing the ship, so by the time the storm arrived everything was secured except the longboard and some loose cargo on the deck. N'duka asked Ghithgod to help him with the rudder, Hazelmere cast heroism on himself, Marlamin cast bless on the rest of us, and then we drove the ship into the storm.

The winds immediately throw the longboat off the deck, but Collins caught it and tied it down. Some ropes on the mainsail snap under the strain and Hazelmere climbed up the mast to repair them. Marlamin was nearly thrown from the ship from the combined blows of several loose objects and a wave washing over the ship. Lightning struck the headsail, lightning in on fire and nearly cracking it in two. Ghithgod and N'duka had been keeping the ship on a straight course, but the cargo in the hold broke lose and started to the ship off course. Lightning strikes the hatches and longboat, the former left mostly intact and the latter completely destroyed. Collins and Marlamin start working to secure and stabilize the headsail, but the cargo in the hold starts damaging the hull and we quickly take on water. Collins left the mast to Marlamin and went into the hold with Hazelmere, where they were able to repair the leaks and secure the cargo. The rudder had been slowly cracking under the strain of the storm, but lightning struck it and severely damaged it. Luckily Ghithgod and N'duka managed to get out of the way in time. The winds also finally cause the headsail to fail and the mast fell onto the deck. Collins, Ghithgod, and N'duka fixed the rudder while Hazelmere and Marlamin cut the mast free. We got the ship turned back around and finally sailed out the far side of the storm.

With our one remaining mast we eventually limped to shore, where N'duka beached the boat to make repairs. We all settled on the ground to recover and rest, but a short while later the first mate approached N'duka and pointed out to sea. We saw a flock of birds circling in the sky, flying over a swirl in the water that was quickly approaching us. N'duka called for everyone to draw steel.
